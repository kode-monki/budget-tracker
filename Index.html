Help & FAQ
Answers to common questions about setting up and using Budget Tracker.
Getting started
What is Budget Tracker and how does it work?▶

Budget Tracker compares approved budgets against actual GL spending, organized by SuiteKey (cost center or project code).

It pulls from two sources:

  • Budget sheet - a Google Sheet created through the app, pre-filled with your chart of accounts. You enter the approved dollar amounts.
  • GL transactions sheet - a Google Sheet where you paste your NetSuite GL Multicurrency export.

The dashboard shows each SuiteKey you are authorized for, with actuals vs. budget, utilization bars, and drill-down to individual transactions.

What do I need to set up before I can use the app?▶

Finance completes these steps first:

1
Create a Permissions sheet with columns: SuiteKey (A), Viewer emails comma-separated (B), Owner email (C). Paste the URL in Settings.
2
Paste the NetSuite chart of accounts export into a Google Sheet and link it in Settings under Chart of accounts.

Then each user does:

3
Paste their NetSuite GL Multicurrency export into a Google Sheet and link it in Settings.
4
Go to Manage, select their FY, and click Create budget for each SuiteKey they own.
Finance sets up the permissions and COA sheets once. Each user links their own GL export independently.
What format does the GL export need to be in?▶

Export the General Ledger Multicurrency report from NetSuite. Expected columns:

  • Col A - Account section header (e.g. 5010 - Product Sales)
  • Col B - Account code on detail rows (e.g. 5010)
  • Col D - Posted date in DD-MM-YY format
  • Col F - Document number
  • Col G - Description
  • Col H - Name / donor / vendor
  • Col K - ICJE SuiteKey
  • Col L - Debit, Col M - Credit, Col O - Local amount
The SuiteKey column (K) must be populated for transactions to appear. Rows without a SuiteKey are ignored.
What format does the chart of accounts need to be in?▶

Export your COA from NetSuite and paste into a Google Sheet. The app reads:

  • Col A - NetSuite Category (e.g. Expense, Personnel)
  • Col B - Account code (e.g. 7405)
  • Col C - Account name (e.g. Travel)
  • Col D - Description (shown as tooltip in the budget template)

Balance sheet accounts (codes below 4000), SUMMARY rows, and header rows are skipped automatically.

Dashboard
What do the colored badges mean?▶
  • Green (e.g. 42%) - on track, under 80% of budget used
  • Yellow (e.g. 85%) - approaching budget, 80% or above
  • Red (Over) - spending has exceeded the approved budget
How do I filter which accounts show on the dashboard?▶

After clicking into a SuiteKey, click Filter accounts in the top right. Checkboxes let you show or hide individual accounts.

By default only accounts with a budget or actual spending are shown. Use the footer buttons:

  • All - show every account in the COA
  • None - hide everything
  • With activity - reset to the default
  • Show all with spending toggle - auto-includes accounts that have transactions even if unchecked
Dots in the filter show status: blue = budget and spending, green = spending only, gray = budget only.
How do I filter by date range?▶

Use the period selector at the top of the dashboard:

  • Full year - entire fiscal year (October to September by default)
  • Quarter - Q1 through Q4
  • Month - any month in your GL data
  • Custom - any from/to date range

The fiscal year start month is set in Code.gs under FISCAL_YEAR_START_MONTH (currently October).

Why does an account show spending but no budget?▶

The account has GL transactions but no budget amount was entered. Common reasons:

  • The budget was left at $0 for that account
  • The account was not in the COA when the budget sheet was created
  • The account code in the GL does not match the code in the budget sheet

Fix: open Budget template and enter an amount, or archive and recreate the budget after updating the COA.

Budgets
Who can create and edit a budget?▶

Only the owner of a SuiteKey (Column C in the permissions sheet) can create and edit its budget. Viewers (Column B) can see the data but cannot edit.

Contact Finance to be added as an owner or viewer for a SuiteKey.
Where is my budget sheet stored?▶

In your personal Google Drive, named Budget - [SuiteKey] - FY[year]. Open it directly from the Budget template tab via the "Open in Sheets" link or from the Manage tab.

How do I create a budget for a new fiscal year?▶
1
Go to the Manage tab
2
Select the new fiscal year from the dropdown
3
Click Create budget next to each SuiteKey you own

All amounts start at $0. Enter your approved amounts in the Budget template tab.

You can have budgets for multiple fiscal years at the same time.
How do I remove an old or incorrect budget?▶

Go to Manage, select the fiscal year, and click Archive and remove. The Google Sheet is renamed to [Archived] Budget - ... and removed from the app. The file stays in your Drive.

To restore an archived budget, rename the file in Drive (remove "[Archived]") then create a new budget for that SuiteKey/FY in Manage - you will need to re-enter the amounts.
Permissions and access
How does the permissions sheet work?▶

Finance maintains one Google Sheet. Each row is one SuiteKey:

  • Col A - SuiteKey (e.g. WaxhawIT-U_SIL)
  • Col B - Comma-separated viewer emails (everyone who can see this SuiteKey)
  • Col C - Single owner email (the one person who can edit the budget)

A person can appear on multiple rows and will see all those SuiteKeys. A manager's supervisor gets access by Finance adding their email to Column B.

Changes take effect immediately on the next page load. No redeployment needed.
I don't see any SuiteKeys on my dashboard. What's wrong?▶

Likely causes:

  • Your email has not been added to the permissions sheet - contact Finance
  • The permissions sheet URL is not configured in Settings - contact your Finance admin
  • You are signed into a different Google account than the one Finance has on file

Check Settings to confirm which email you are signed in as (shown at the bottom).

Troubleshooting
The dashboard loads but shows $0 for everything. Why?▶

Check:

  • Settings - confirm your GL sheet URL is saved and the correct tab is selected
  • The ICJE SuiteKey column (K) in your GL export matches the SuiteKey exactly, including capitalization
  • The selected date range covers a period that has transactions in your GL
The app is slow to load. What can I do?▶

Load time is mainly affected by:

  • GL sheet size - keep your GL export to a single fiscal year per tab
  • Number of SuiteKeys - each authorized SuiteKey opens a budget file. More SuiteKeys = longer load.
  • Cold start - the first load after inactivity is slower. Subsequent loads are faster.
How do I update the app after a new version is released?▶
1
Paste updated Code.gs and Index.html into the Apps Script editor and save
2
Deploy > Manage deployments > existing deployment > New version > Deploy

The URL never changes. All users get the new version automatically on next load.

Who do I contact for help?▶
  • Access issues (missing SuiteKeys, can't see a budget) - contact Finance
  • Technical issues (app errors, slow loading) - contact your IT administrator
  • Budget questions (wrong amounts, missing accounts) - contact the budget owner shown on each SuiteKey card
